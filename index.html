Security you can inspect

The cryptography that protects your seed is open source under Apache 2.0. The full threat model + subsystem writeup lives in the public repo. Read it. Audit it. File a PR.

  • Android Keystore / iOS Keychain binds every seed to hardware
  • One biometric prompt per session cache
  • Best-effort memory zeroing after every signing + KDF call
  • allowBackup="false" on Android — no accidental cloud copies
  • PBKDF2 → HKDF-SHA-256 → AES-256-CBC for on-device notebook data
  • No server-side custody, ever. Backend never sees a seed.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

Is Stellar TimeLock a non-custodial wallet? +

Yes. Your seed phrase and all encrypted notebook data live only on your device, protected by the Android Keystore or iOS Keychain. Our backend never sees your keys, your ciphertext, or any personally identifiable information about your wallet activity.

How do time-locked savings vaults work? +

Each vault is a call to our public Soroban smart contract on Stellar mainnet. You deposit XLM and choose an unlock date up to 1,000 days in the future. Once created, no one — not even Stellar TimeLock — can withdraw before that date. There is no admin key, no emergency exit, and no upgrade path.

What is memo protection? +

Exchanges and custodial platforms require a numeric or text memo to credit your deposit. If you paste a destination that our address book flags as an exchange or a casino, the wallet refuses to sign the transaction until you add a memo. This blocks the single most common cause of permanently lost Stellar Lumens.

Do I have to use the crypto features? +

No. During onboarding you can pick a productivity-only mode that hides the Crypto tab entirely. You still get the encrypted notes, bill calendar, P&L ledger, and built-in authenticator, and you never see a Stellar address unless you opt in later from Settings.

What encryption does the notebook use? +

Notebook data uses PBKDF2 to stretch your wallet-derived material, HKDF-SHA-256 to split subsystem keys, and AES-256-CBC for encryption. Every note, bill, ledger entry, and authenticator secret is sealed before it hits AsyncStorage. Even the Google Drive backup blob is ciphertext — the cloud sees an opaque envelope, never plaintext.

How is the app funded? +

The productivity core (notes, bill calendar, ledger, authenticator, calculator) is available as a small monthly subscription. All wallet features — send, receive, swap, and time-lock vaults — are free forever. We never take a protocol fee on your transactions.

Where can I audit the code? +

The client-side cryptography and the Soroban vault contract are both public under Apache 2.0 in the stellartimelock-core repository. Issues, pull requests, and responsible disclosures are welcome.
