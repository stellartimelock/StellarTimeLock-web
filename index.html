Vault Types

Four ways to lock assets on Stellar mainnet. All non-custodial. All enforced by Soroban smart contracts with no admin key.

  • ⏳

    Standard Time-Lock

    Lock XLM or any Stellar token until a specific date up to 1,000 days out. Simple, immutable, no early withdrawal.

  • ❄️

    Cooling-Off Lock

    Withdraw anytime — but funds release only after a delay you set (24 hours to 7 days). Cancel the withdrawal during the countdown. Protection against impulse moves and unauthorized access.

  • ⏲️

    Vesting Schedule

    Lock a lump sum that unlocks gradually over time. 1,200 USDC over 12 months = 100 USDC available each month. Perfect for founders, teams, and disciplined savings plans.

  • 💓

    Dead Man's Switch

    Name a legacy contact and set an inactivity period. If you don't check in before the deadline, your contact can claim the funds. Inheritance planning without a lawyer.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

Is Stellar TimeLock a non-custodial wallet? +

Yes. Your seed phrase and all encrypted notebook data live only on your device, protected by the Android Keystore or iOS Keychain. Our backend infrastructure never sees your keys, your ciphertext, or any personally identifiable information.

How do time-locked vaults work? +

Each vault is a call to a public Soroban smart contract on Stellar mainnet. You deposit XLM or any Stellar token and choose an unlock date. Once created, no one — not even Stellar TimeLock — can withdraw before that date. There is no admin key, no emergency exit, and no upgrade path.

What is a Cooling-Off Lock? +

A Cooling-Off Lock lets you initiate a withdrawal at any time, but funds are only released after a delay you choose (24 hours to 7 days). During the countdown, you can cancel the withdrawal. If someone gains unauthorized access to your wallet, you have the full delay window to stop the transfer.

What is a Dead Man's Switch? +

You set a backup address and an inactivity period. You must periodically check in by tapping "Ping." If you don't check in before the deadline, your designated contact can claim the funds. It's non-custodial inheritance planning — no lawyers, no probate.

What is memo protection? +

Exchanges and custodial platforms require a numeric or text memo to credit your deposit. If you paste a destination that our address book flags as an exchange, the wallet refuses to sign until you add a memo. This blocks the single most common cause of permanently lost Stellar Lumens.

Do I have to use the crypto features? +

No. During onboarding you can pick a productivity-only mode that hides the Crypto tab entirely. You still get encrypted notes, bill calendar, P&L ledger, and built-in authenticator, and you never see a Stellar address unless you opt in later.

How is the app funded? +

The Basic tier is free forever — all productivity features, swaps, and up to 10 vaults. Plus ($25/month) and Unlimited ($75/month) tiers are for power users and institutions who need more vaults and advanced features. Swap affiliate fees also contribute. No ads, no data selling, no protocol fees on your transactions.

Where can I audit the code? +

The client-side cryptography and the Soroban vault contracts are all public under Apache 2.0 in the stellartimelock-core repository. Issues, pull requests, and responsible disclosures are welcome.
